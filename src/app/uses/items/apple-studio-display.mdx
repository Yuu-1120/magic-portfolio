## Overview

The Apple Studio Display is my primary external monitor, paired with the MacBook Pro 16" in clamshell mode. The 5K Retina display delivers stunning color accuracy and sharpness that makes both design work and code reading a pleasure.

## Specifications

- **Resolution:** 5120 x 2880 (5K Retina)
- **Size:** 27-inch
- **Brightness:** 600 nits
- **Color:** P3 wide color, True Tone
- **Camera:** 12MP Ultra Wide with Center Stage
- **Audio:** Six-speaker system with Spatial Audio
- **Connectivity:** Thunderbolt 3 (USB-C), 3x USB-C

## Why This Display

### Color Accuracy

Out of the box, the Studio Display is factory calibrated with exceptional Delta E values. For design work in Figma and Photoshop, I rarely need to second-guess color decisions. The P3 wide color gamut means I can see colors that most monitors simply can't reproduce.

### Text Clarity

At 5K resolution on a 27-inch panel, text rendering is incredibly crisp — similar to a MacBook's built-in Retina display. When you're staring at code all day, this makes a real difference. Fonts look sharp even at small sizes, and anti-aliasing is minimal.

### Center Stage Camera

The built-in 12MP camera with Center Stage is great for video calls. It keeps you centered in the frame even if you move around, which is surprisingly useful during standup meetings.

## Setup Notes

I connect the MacBook Pro via Thunderbolt, which simultaneously charges the laptop at 96W and provides a single-cable setup. The three additional USB-C ports on the back handle my webcam, USB hub, and occasional external drive.

## Daily Usage

- **Coding:** Two-column layout — editor on the left, terminal/browser on the right
- **Design:** Full-width Figma canvas at 100% zoom with room for tool panels
- **Meetings:** Center Stage camera handles video calls without needing a separate webcam
- **Media:** The six-speaker system is surprisingly good for music and podcasts during work
