## Overview

The Logitech MX Master 3S has been my daily driver mouse for over a year. Its ergonomic shape, electromagnetic scroll wheel, and seamless multi-device switching make it ideal for long work sessions that alternate between coding, design, and communication.

## Specifications

- **Sensor:** 8000 DPI Darkfield (works on any surface)
- **Scroll:** MagSpeed electromagnetic wheel (ratchet + hyper-fast)
- **Buttons:** 7 programmable buttons
- **Connectivity:** Bluetooth + Logi Bolt receiver
- **Battery:** Up to 70 days, USB-C quick charge
- **Weight:** 141g
- **Compatibility:** macOS, Windows, Linux, iPadOS

## Why This Mouse

### Ergonomics

The sculpted shape fits my right hand perfectly. After switching from a flat mouse, wrist fatigue during long coding sessions virtually disappeared. The thumb rest and contoured grip mean I can work 8+ hours without discomfort.

### MagSpeed Scroll Wheel

The electromagnetic scroll wheel is a game-changer:

- **Ratchet mode:** Precise line-by-line scrolling for reading code and documentation
- **Free-spin mode:** Flick the wheel and it scrolls 1000 lines per second — perfect for navigating large files or long design canvases
- **Horizontal scroll:** The thumb wheel handles horizontal scrolling in Figma, wide spreadsheets, and timeline editors

### Multi-Device Workflow

I pair the mouse with both my MacBook Pro and iPad. A single button press on the bottom switches between devices instantly. Logitech Flow even lets me copy-paste between the two machines by simply moving the cursor to the edge of the screen.

### Quiet Clicks

The "S" in 3S stands for "Silent." The main buttons are incredibly quiet — about 90% quieter than the original MX Master 3. This is perfect for shared workspaces and late-night coding sessions.

## My Button Configuration

Using Logi Options+, I've customized the buttons:

| Button | Function |
|--------|----------|
| Side button 1 (forward) | Mission Control |
| Side button 2 (back) | Switch desktops |
| Thumb wheel | Horizontal scroll / zoom in Figma |
| Gesture button | Custom gestures (app switcher, notification center) |
| DPI button | Toggle between 800 DPI (design) and 1600 DPI (code) |

## Battery Life

With typical use (8 hours/day), I charge it once every 2 months. A 3-minute quick charge gives enough power for a full day — so running out of battery is never a real concern.

## Comparison: Trackpad vs MX Master 3S

While the MacBook Pro trackpad is excellent, the MX Master 3S offers:

- Faster navigation across large codebases
- More precise selection in design tools
- Reduced wrist strain from repetitive gestures
- Dedicated scroll wheel for code review and documentation
